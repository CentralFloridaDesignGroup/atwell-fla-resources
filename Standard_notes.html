Surveyor's Notes

Surveyor's notes are a critical part of every product. They clarify Surveyor's intent, show compliance with statutory requirements, and provide a place for information that cannot go on the map. The language behind surveyor's notes are purposefully written to maintain as little liability on the Surveyor, and can be rather confusing on what they mean. This page is meant to help teach new technicians and surveyors the why behind notes. This page also has certain words colored in a orange color to help draw your focus to what may change from survey to survey.

Signature Requirements

Maps of Survey
THIS MAP OF BOUNDARY SURVEY IS NOT VALID UNLESS IT BEARS THE SIGNATURE AND SEAL OF THE FLORIDA LICENSED SURVEYOR AND MAPPER IDENTIFIED BELOW.
Sketch and Descriptions
THIS LEGAL DESCRIPTION IS NOT VALID IF PRINTED, OR WITHOUT THE ORIGINAL SIGNATURE AND RAISED SEAL OF THE PROFESSIONAL SURVEYOR AND MAPPER SHOWN HEREON.
Type of Survey

This note is required per §5J-17.051(3)(e), Florida Administrative Code, which states:

(e) The survey map and report and the copies of the survey map and report, except those with electronic signature and electronic seal, must contain a statement indicating that the survey map and report or the copies thereof are not valid without the original signature and seal of a Florida licensed surveyor and mapper.

This note is also incorporated into the signing block itself along with the reference to a digital signature as required in § 5J-17.062, FAC.

The Sketch and Description note varies slightly by just stating that we are only certifying the legal description and not the sketch.

Abstraction

NO ABSTRACT FOR RIGHTS-OF-WAY, EASEMENTS, OWNERSHIP OR OTHER INSTRUMENTS OF RECORD HAVE BEEN PROVIDED TO THIS FIRM.

Surveyors are not land title agents and we have no obligation to do any research of public records when preparing a survey. It is a protection note in the event that, if a easement, right-of-way, or other encumbrance is discovered that affects the land, we are not liable for it's absence.

Do not use if we received any title work for the project, either through an ALTA request or through a Property Information Report.

Vertical Datum

A generic note stating which vertical datum system the survey is tied to and which specific datum benchmark we tied to. 95% of all surveys completed today are in NAVD88, however you still have cities and jurisdictions that are operating in NGVD29. Very rarely will a arbitrary datum be established, which doesn't tie to any public system. The relevant code to reference for this is 5J-17.051(2)(a):

Vertical Accuracy: All surveying and mapping products with elevation data shall state the datum and a description of the control upon which the surveying and mapping products are based. ... Elevation data may be obtained on an assumed datum provided the base elevation of the assumed datum is obviously different than a publicly published datum and clearly noted.

Note that the requirement starts off by saying with elevation data, meaning if the survey does not have any vertical information (such as a mortgage survey), then we do not need to specify a vertical datum.

Vertical Datum methodology

THE VERTICAL CONTROL ESTABLISHED FOR THIS SURVEY IS BASED ON A CLOSED LEVEL LOOP, HAVING A CLOSURE ACCURACY WHICH MEETS OR EXCEEDS THAT REQUIRED BY THE STANDARDS OF PRACTICE SET FORTH IN RULE CHAPTER 5J-17, FLORIDA ADMINISTRATIVE CODE.

The vertical datum code has three sub-requirements in it. The "Elevations" section handles the first and third requirements. The middle requirement not handled in that said note requires documentation to prove we are accurate. The middle part of .051(2)(a) states the following:

The data shall be supported with documentation describing the vertical accuracy and the methodology used to determine accuracy.

This note is just a note to satisfy that second requirement.
